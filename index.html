About Me

I am a PhD candidate in Computer Science (Data Science & Applied Mathematics concentration) at Tecnologico de Monterrey (ITESM), where I conduct research in the Bioinformatics Research Group, under the supervision of Dr. Victor Trevino. My research focuses on the development of bioinformatic tools, specifically decision trees capable of handling multi-objective and multi-omics data, for cancer subtyping and biomarker discovery. I previously completed a Master’s in Public Health (MPH) in Epidemiology and Biostatistics from Columbia University’s Mailman School of Public Health (2018) and a Bachelor’s of Science (BSc) in Psychology and Neuroscience from the University of Massachusetts Amherst Commonwealth Honors College (2016, Magna Cum Lauda).
